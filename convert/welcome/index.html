￼￼What We’ll Do For You

A/B Tests

Your average number of tests per month depends on our agreement, the scope of the test (for example, a headline change is easier to create than a wholesale rework or ground-up HTML/CSS development), the amount of traffic you get to that particular page, the conclusiveness of the test’s ￼findings, and the impact of that test across other parts of your conversion funnel (if any).

We’re very happy to run multiple tests on multiple pages at once—for instance, your home page and a signup page—but you should never run multiple tests on the same page at once, because it’ll confound (and prolong) the findings of each.

A test can also contain multiple variations of a page, all measured against the same set of goals. For instance, if you change a headline and a call to action, you’ll want four variations of that page, in order to determine the best permutation:

  • Control.
  • Headline.
  • Call to action.
  • Headline + Call to action.

If you change a subheader as well, you’ll want eight variations:

  • Control.
  • Headline.
  • Subheader.
  • Call to action.
  • Headline + Call to action.
  • Headline + Subheader.
  • Subheader + Call to action.
  • Headline + Subheader + Call to action.

￼In sum, if you make n disparate changes to a page at once, you’ll want to serve 2n variations, with 1⁄2n of total traffic going to each variation.

As a result, we take care in making sure we’re choosing the best things to test each time.

We run tests for as long as it takes to determine whether a given variation will be successful, or whether the test will be inconclusive for the foreseeable future.

We run each test for a minimum of two weeks to ensure valid data.

We check in on all Convert tests every week. With each test we determine whether a variation won, or whether we should keep it running a little while longer.

While a test runs, you shouldn’t make any edits to the test page. This could impact our findings in unpredictable ways.

When a test has completed, we’ll write a mini report with our thoughts on why the test won or lost. This information helps us in determining what to test next.

Monthly Reports

We’ll write up a monthly report that assesses our most recent progress, overall takeaways, and proposes a new round of tests to conduct in the following month.

Reports are posted to Trello, and they’ll occasionally require action on your part, in case we have questions or want to roll successful changes out to every visitor.

  • Large-scale redesigns. Actually, we can do one of those, but it would be outside the scope of our typical Convert engagement. If you’re curious about how we’ve planned larger projects with Convert clients, read on!

We’ll be using a few tools to roll out tests, make changes, and coordinate our work together.

We’ll use Trello to post reports, coordinate on future tests, notify each other of our availability, and critique our work together. Trello is a great email substitute, because it ensures a long-lasting, group-visible record of all our work together.

I’m happy to invite as many people as appropriate to Trello; just let me know their email addresses. (You can also invite them yourself.)

Convert.com and Optimizely.com

Unless you have your own A/B testing account, we’ll use one of our accounts at Convert.com or Optimizely.com.

You’ll be given access to the account to view test results, but we’ll have access to make changes to the tests themselves.

You’ll be required to add a snippet of JavaScript to all pages that you want to test. Please do this before we kick off our first test.

Analytics tools

Before we begin our first A/B test, we’d like to be granted access to your analytics tools.

You can give Google Analytics access to hello@davidcrowther.com and emils@davidcrowther.com.

Access to other analytics suites (KISSmetrics, Mixpanel, Mint, etc.) also use hello@davidcrowther.com and emils@davidcrowther.com.

This is so we can determine how your conversion funnels are working, and what pages are getting enough traffic to perform statistically significant tests. We pay careful attention to your analytics as we put together tests and take action on future changes.

Rolling out changes

After enough time, we’ll have a successful test, and we’ll want to roll a variant’s changes out to everyone. This means we’ll have to make changes to the site itself.

We’ve found that things move much faster if you give me GitHub, Subversion, WordPress admin, and/or FTP access to your site, so we can make these changes as needed.

If you don’t want to give us this kind of access, I understand, but do keep in mind that our ability to move forward will be constrained by your ability to roll changes out quickly.

Either way, we’ll keep you apprised of all successful tests and their attendant changes on Trello.

A Few Questions You Might Have

We get over 50,000 visitors per month. Is Convert a good fit for us?

Yes! Any site can be A/B tested, and it’s great that you’re already gathering a ton of valuable data. That said, Convert.com and Optimizely.com monthly plans limit the number of visitors that we can test with, and we’re operating under that constraint when we work with everyone. There are a couple of ways we could take care of this:

  • Limit the proportion of users that see a test. This can reduce the number of overall hits we get billed for. The downside of this, of course, is that a test will take that much longer.
  • Sign up for a separate A/B testing account, and give me access to it. This will result in more, better, and faster tests, but you will have to pay for it. At your scale, you might want to speak with Convert.com or Optimizely.com for a larger plan. Both of them have great plans that support over 100,000 page views per month.

I don’t want to use Convert.com or Optimizely.com. Can we go with a different A/B testing app?

Sure, but Convert comes with Convert.com or Optimizely.com for free, not any other testing framework. If you want to use a different framework or a larger plan, you’ll need to pay for and maintain it yourself. We’ll create the tests in whatever you’re happiest using, just give us access and we can take it from there.

Why doesn’t Convert.com or Optimizely.com’s reported data match my internal analytics?

There are a lot of technical reasons for this, which is partly why Convert.com, Optimizely.com and other A/B testing suites work in terms of statistical confidence. Tests that finish with 90% confidence still have a 10% chance of not providing some measurable improvement.

Can we test mobile sites as well?

Yes! We love testing mobile and responsive sites. But testing native apps is outside of scope.

 I noticed you increased your rates on Convert’s home page. Will I pay that much someday?

No. All Convert clients are grandfathered in at whatever rate they started at. Our rate is based on the value that we provide most of our clients, so it might change for future clients. Higher rates also allow us to develop a better, more stable set of clients.

Please note: if you want to put our work on hold for a few months, when we begin working together again, we’ll ask for whatever the current rate is. We believe that’s the fairest policy for my business and our other clients.

But won’t I get worse service than somebody who pays more?

No. We treat everybody the same: again, it’s about being fair to all of our clients. We work as hard as we can for all of our Convert clients.
