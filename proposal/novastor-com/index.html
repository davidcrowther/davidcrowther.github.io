Continuous small improvements to the copy and design of your website can make major and lasting improvements to your sales and revenue.

Called Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO), this process consists of analyzing, updating and testing changes to your website to increase your conversion rates.

A/B testing really is a simple concept. Create two (or more) different versions of a page on your website, measure which performs best, and then implement the winning version.

“Companies are spending too much money on acquiring customers. Successful conversion means maximizing the value from every customer visit and ensuring they always come back for more.”
– Dr. Tal Schwartz, Founder and CEO, ClickTale

This is what your competition knows that you don’t:

  • Increasing your conversion rates increases your profit without spending more on advertising.
  • Having higher conversion rates opens up new advertising channels that weren’t available to you before because of your increased return on advertising spend (ROAS).

But how do you know what to change? What would you change it to? And how can you measure whether those changes were effective?

That’s where I come in.

My Process to Increase Your Conversions:

1. Evaluate

First we discover (not guess) which parts of your business are under-performing and why. We analyse your analytics, your sales funnel, perform usability tests, talk to your customers, talk to your team and review your competition.

Having a “fresh pair of eyes” shouldn’t be underestimated in this crucial stage.

We then define your business goals and how they translate into your website goals, and decide how you’ll measure success. We need to define the key performance indicators (KPIs) that will ensure you meet your goals.

2. Plan

Next we create your optimization strategy. We write a list of A/B tests we want to preform, then prioritize it to pick the low hanging fruit first.

3. Testing

We can talk about theories and best practices all we want, but until we actually test what we create, we’ve no idea if we’re moving the needle forward or backward.

So for every update I make to your copy and/or design, I run an A/B test between the original version and the new.

If I’ve created something new that didn’t exist before, we monitor your higher end goals to measure improvement. This is the only way to be sure the changes are successful and is why I’m happy to offer a guarantee on all my work (see more on this below).

To run these tests on your website I use services like convert.com and optimizely.com.

4. Review & Repeat

After testing comes the review. Did the changes make a positive improvement?

If yes, great! We move onto the next area of the sales funnel we want to improve and start at the beginning of this process again.

If not, we dig deep into the results and uncover the reasons why. We then take these lessons and apply them as we start the process over again.
