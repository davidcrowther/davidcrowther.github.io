Why work with me?

Because unlike other designers and agencies, my focus is on creating a user friendly design that not only looks great, but also improves revenue generated through your website.

My ultimate goal for every project is to increase your websites conversion rate.

A quick overview of my Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) design process is as follows:

1. Research and Analysis:

Define your business and website goals, evaluate your website, study the information you send me, search the web for additional information, analyse your website analytics and review your competitors websites.

2. Information Architecture:

Information architecture is the art and science of figuring out what you want your site to do and then constructing a plan of how we’ll achieve that goal. It includes creating a site map and user journeys (flowcharts) to craft your ideal sales funnel.

3. Direct Response Copywriting

Great copy is the workhorse behind your conversions and this paired with the right design can amplify your conversions with a cumulative effect.

4. Design:

Based on my research and analysis, I create wireframes that outline the structure and layout of your new pages. I then work with my expert team of designers to design each new page based on my wireframes and copy.

5. Development

Each design is carefully crafted pixel by pixel into clean HTML, CSS and Javascript code and then tested across the four main browsers (Safari, Firefox, Chrome & IE). We also offer ‘Responsive design’ as standard so that your pages look great on tablet and mobile devices.

Recommendations:

As you know I don’t believe WordPress is the right platform for your requirements.

While well known, WordPress is not the default answer for all web projects.

For this project I would recommend using Webhook which allows for fast and flexible development.

It’s a hybrid of sorts in the fact that it’s Open Source, but offers paid hosted and managed version for $9 per month. Don’t think having your hosting and CMS managed is insignificant—people always underestimate the time and costs involved in maintaining a server and the software installed on it.

It allows you and your team to edit content like a normal CMS, but then saves a ‘static’ version of the website for speed and stability.

A ‘static’ website is just HTML files and images, and doesn’t use a database or backend programming language like PHP. This means less things to go wrong between your website and the visitor viewing it.

And you can’t get faster than static websites.

We can integrate the Ontraport subscriber and application forms within the Webhook CMS no problem.
