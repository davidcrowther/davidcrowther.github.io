Proposal: mortgagethoughts.co.uk

Created for Luke Saint by David Crowther

I been looking in more depth around your website at mortgagethoughts.co.uk and you’ve done a great job with the current copy and design.

Sure we could tweak it and squeeze some more conversions out of it, but the trouble is that you’re traffic is to low for A/B testing.

So I recommend your next priority would be to focus on generating more traffic. I suggest content marketing (blogging) and of course PPC.

For PPC let’s start with Google AdWords and then test Bing Ads, Facebook Ads and LinkedIn Ads.

So we took a look at your current Google AdWords campaign, and we’d focus on the following two areas:

  1. Campaign structure: optimise for improved quality score for better CPC, ad position, etc. The goal here is to lower your ad spend, or at least increase clicks with the current spend (ideally both).
  2. Conversion tracking: there are at least 2 different forms on the landing page which should be tracked separately. We also highly recommend setting up call tracking as a large portion of your conversions are by phone.

Measuring success can be difficult as campaigns can be managed certain ways to meet certain metric goals.

Some agencies guarantee their work, but then use the following ‘cheats’ to achieve their results:

  1. Guarantee to increase your click through rate.
    Cheat: pause the low CTR keywords.

  2. Guarantee to get you more clicks.
    Cheat: bid higher or on more keywords—even if those higher bids or extra keywords aren’t profitable.

  3. Guarantee to lower your cost per click.
    Cheat: lower the bids.

  4. Guarantee to increase your %ROI.
    Cheat: drop the stuff making the lowest profit.

  5. Guarantee to increase your number of sales.
    Cheat: bid higher or on more keywords—even if those higher bids or extra keywords aren’t profitable.

  6. Guarantee to cut your cost per conversion.
    Cheat: drop the stuff that has the highest cost per conversion (even if it’s profitable), lower bids so fewer (but cheaper) clicks.

Ultimately, the most important metric is if you (the client) is happy and that your campaign is providing you a good return on investment.
