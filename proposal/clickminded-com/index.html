Continuous improvements to the copy and design of your landing page can make major and lasting improvements to your sales and revenue.

If you don’t know already, Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO), is the process of analyzing, updating and testing changes to your website to increase your conversion rates.

A part of CRO is A/B testing in which we create two (or more) different versions of your landing page, measure which performs best, and then implement the winning version.

“Testing works. If you’re not doing it well, you’re missing out. Your website can (and should) get better. Every single day.” – Seth Godin, Author & Marketing Guru

But how do you know what to change? What would you change it to? And how can you measure whether those changes were effective?

That’s where I come in.

My Process to Increase Your Conversions:

1. Research and Analysis:

I study the information you send me, search the web for additional information, analyse your website and review your competitors websites.

2. Direct Response Copywriting

Great copy is the workhorse behind your conversions and this paired with the right design can amplify your conversions with a cumulative effect.

3. Design:

Based on my research and analysis, I create wireframes that outline the structure and layout of your new page/s. I then work with my team of designers to design the new page.

4. Testing:

We can talk about theories and best practices all we want, but until we actually test what we create, we’ve no idea if we’re moving the needle forward.

So I run an A/B test between the original version and the new. If I’ve created something new that didn’t exist before, we monitor your higher end goals to measure improvement. This is the only way to be sure the changes are successful and is why I’m happy to offer a guarantee on all my work (see more on this below).

To run these tests on your website I use services like http://convert.com and https://www.optimizely.com

How do we tell if the new design worked?

For those who have over 5,000 visitors per month, we run what’s called an A/B test between the old design and the new design. When we run the test, the traffic is split evenly between the old and new landing pages and any conversions are recorded.

We try again. I analyse the results of the first A/B test and create a new design. This is all included and free of charge. If we repetitively fail to increase your conversion rate (which hasn’t happened yet) then we offer you the 80% refund.
