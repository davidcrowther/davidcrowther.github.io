This is my full service solution.

I don’t just examine your website – instead I look at your whole online business system and every online interaction you have with your customers.

Here’s my process broken down:

1. Evaluate

First we discover (not guess) which parts of your business are under-performing and why. We analyse your analytics, your sales funnel, perform usability tests, talk to your customers, talk to your team and review your competition.

You’ve been so evolved with your website that you become blind to it’s faults. Having a “fresh pair of eyes” shouldn’t be underestimated in this crucial stage.

We then define your business goals and how they translate into your website goals, and decide how you’ll measure success. We need to define the key performance indicators (KPIs) that will ensure you meet your goals.

2. Plan

Next we create your optimization strategy. We define your target market, advertising channels to test, and the ideal visitor path through your website.

We write up a staged plan of attack where we’re looking to plug existing leaks in your sales funnel as well as open new avenues for increased traffic and retention.

3. Content

If I could only change one thing on a website to increase it’s conversions, it would be the copy. The words on your page will have more impact on a visitors decision to take action than any other element on the page.

Not to say that good design is not important – in fact great copy paired with the right design can amplify your conversions with a cumulative effect.

Having a content strategy ensures the right message is shown consistency across the whole of your sales funnel.

4. Create

Following our plan and working with you and your team, I create the required copy, wireframes, and/or designs for the first stage of your optimization plan.

We might be creating an email campaign, writing PPC ads to split test or redesigning your home page – whatever is required in your optimization strategy to plug the holes and increase your revenues.

If any development is required, we use the popular Bootstrap framework from the team at Twitter. All our development is ‘responsive’ so that your pages look great on tablets and mobiles.

5. Testing

We can talk about theories and best practices all we want, but until we actually test what we create, we’ve no idea if we’re moving the needle forward or backward.

So for every change I make, we run an A/B test between the original version and the new. If we’ve created something new that didn’t exist before, we monitor your higher end goals to measure improvement. This is the only way to be sure the changes are successful and is why I’m happy to offer an 80% money back guarantee on all my work (see more on this below).

6. Review

After testing comes the review. Did the changes make a positive improvement?

If yes, great! We move onto the next area of the sales funnel we want to improve and start at the beginning of this process again.

If not, we dig deep into the results and uncover the reasons why. We then take these lessons and apply them as we start the process over again.
