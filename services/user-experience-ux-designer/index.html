DAVID CROWTHER

User Experience (UX) Designer

Let me tell you a story about two business men called Johnny Wright and Larry de Losser.

Now, both Johnny and Larry sold eyewear for canines - nothing but the finest quality glasses for the most discerning of pooches.

They were both looking to build their businesses and become the top dog (pardon the pun) in their industry.

For his website peek-a-pooch.com, Larry decided to use one of those fancy design agencies that talk about ‘breaking the rules’ and ‘pushing the envelope’ because Larry thought he was the type of guy that pushes envelopes…

Johnny, on the other hand, hired an experienced (and, dare I say, handsome) User Experience (UX) consultant to design his website designer-doggy.com with a strong focus on usability.

After all, he didn’t want his site to just look good, he wanted people to be able to use it, no scratch that, he wanted people to love using it.

While Larry’s agency was busy ‘making the complex simple’ and ‘creating ideas that capture people’s imaginations’, Johnny’s consultant researched and analyised Johnny’s current website along with his competitors.

He looked at Johnny’s website analytics, surveyed his customers and planned how he would design Johnny’s new website to increase the sales of doggy glasses.

The time came when both Johnny’s and Larry’s new websites were launched.

Larry’s average sale was worth $100 and as his old website had a conversion rate of 1%, he was making one sale out of every 100 visitors to his website.

Larry’s new website looked flash but took a bit longer to download. The copy and imagery was certainly original, but it was so original that some people weren’t sure how to use the site. Nether were they impressed with the clever headlines – they just wanted to look at glasses for their dog.

Larry’s sales didn’t improve, in fact his conversion rate dropped.

Johnny’s new wedsite design also looked good. It looked professional, but also clean and simple (which is harder to do than you think). Visitors instantly knew how to navigate around the site and it seemed that the information about their favorite doggy glasses was within easy reach.

Johnny’s conversion rate doubled from one sale for every 100 visitors to two sales for every 100 visitors.

Johnny was now making double the revenue per month and was ecstatically happy.

Now … who would you rather be?
